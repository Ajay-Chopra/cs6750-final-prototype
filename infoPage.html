How Article Scores are Calculated

Accuracy

An article's Accuracy score is determined by the percentage of its statements that are truthful. For instance, if an article makes 10 claims and 8 of the claims are true, then the article will have an accuracy score of 80%

Sources

An article's Sources score is determined by the percentage of its claims that contain cited sources and the accuracy scores of each of those sources. Hence, the sources must be cited and must be reliable in order for an article to attain a high sources score.

Human Written

The Human Written score is the percentage liklihood that the article was written by a human as opposed to generative AI. Our models are trained on millions of examples of both human written and AI written articles and can distinguish between them with >95% accuracy.


Annotation Colors and Meanings

Color Description
Yellow Statement that may be technically true but is presented in a misleading way.
Teal A community note left by other readers and verified by fact checking
Red Statement that is factually incorrect.
